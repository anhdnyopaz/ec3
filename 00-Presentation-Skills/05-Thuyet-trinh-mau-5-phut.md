> **Mục đích:** 3 bài thuyết trình hoàn chỉnh **~5 phút (≈600–650 từ)** cho 3 chủ đề:
> 1. **AI đã thay đổi công việc & sự nghiệp của giới trẻ như thế nào** *(liên quan [03-AI-Workplace-Education](../03-AI-Workplace-Education/) & [Career-Issues](../Career-Issues/))*
> 2. **Hoạt động giải trí mạo hiểm — ưu điểm & nhược điểm** *(liên quan [02-Entertainment](../02-Entertainment/))*

>
> **Quy ước in chữ:** cụm từ **khung thuyết trình** (học ở File 1–3) in **đậm** · _từ vựng chủ đề_ in _nghiêng_.
> **Cách dùng:** (1) Đọc to cảm nhận nhịp → (2) Học **ý chính**, không học vẹt → (3) Thay ví dụ bằng chuyện của BẠN → (4) Nói lại không nhìn giấy, quên thì dùng câu cứu nguy ở File 3.

## ⏱️ Phân bổ thời gian 5 phút (≈600–650 từ nói)

| Phần | Thời gian | Nội dung |
|---|---|---|
| 👋 Mở đầu + câu hỏi gây chú ý + bố cục | ~45 giây | Chào, hook question, quan điểm, 3 phần |
| 1️⃣ Luận điểm 1 | ~60–70 giây | 2–3 ý nhỏ + 1 ví dụ |
| 2️⃣ Luận điểm 2 | ~60–70 giây | 2–3 ý nhỏ + 1 số liệu/ví dụ |
| 3️⃣ Luận điểm 3 | ~60–70 giây | 2 ý nhỏ + 1 câu chuyện cá nhân |
| ⚖️ Nhượng bộ + lời khuyên | ~30 giây | Of course… However… + advice |
| 🙏 Kết luận + cảm ơn | ~30 giây | Nhắc 3 ý + thông điệp chính + mời câu hỏi |

> 💡 **Khác biệt lớn nhất so với bài 2 phút:** mỗi luận điểm phải "sâu" hơn (2–3 ý nhỏ, có ví dụ riêng),
> và bạn cần **nhiều câu chuyển ý hơn** để người nghe không bị lạc. Nói CHẬM — 5 phút là marathon, không phải chạy nước rút.

# ✍️ BÀI 1 — *How AI Has Changed Careers for Young People* (≈620 từ)

> Good morning everyone, and thank you for being here today. My name is ______, and I'm very happy to see you all.

> Before I start, let me ask you a question: **Have you ever wondered** what your job will look like in ten years? Maybe your future job doesn't even exist yet! **That's what I'll talk about today** — how AI has changed _careers_ for young people. **In my opinion**, AI has changed our working life in three big ways, and young people need to understand them now, not later.

> **I've divided my talk into three parts. First, I'll talk about** the new _opportunities_ that AI brings. **Then, I'll move on to** the new _skills_ we need. **And finally, I'll discuss** the risks and how to prepare for them. After that, **I'd be happy to answer** your questions.

> **Let's begin by looking at** the opportunities. **The main reason is that** AI creates many jobs that did not exist five years ago. **For example**, companies today need _prompt engineers_ — people who know how to talk to AI — and _AI trainers_, who teach AI systems to work better. **What's more**, AI helps young people start their own business. **Because** AI tools can design a logo, write an advertisement, and answer customers, one student can now do the work of a small team. **Take** my cousin, **for example**. Last year, he opened an online shop alone. AI writes his product descriptions, so he can focus on his customers. **The numbers tell us that** this is not a small trend: **according to a survey**, more than half of young workers already use AI tools every week.

> **Now that we've looked at** the opportunities, **let's turn to** my second point: skills. **Since** AI can do simple tasks quickly and cheaply, the _job market_ now wants different things from us. **The key point here is** that we don't have to be better than AI — we have to be good at working WITH it. **In my opinion**, there are three skills every young person should learn. First, using AI tools — this is becoming as important as English. Second, _critical thinking_, **because** AI sometimes gives wrong answers, so we must check everything. And third, _soft skills_ like communication and teamwork, **because** these are things AI cannot copy.

> **That brings me to my last point:** the risks. **Of course**, AI can _replace_ some jobs, especially simple and repeated work like _data entry_ or basic translation. **Let me share a personal story.** My friend had a part-time job as a translator. Last year, her company started using AI, and she lost that job. At first, she was very sad. **However**, she didn't give up. She learned to edit and improve AI translations, and now she earns more than before. **What really matters is** how we react to change.

> I know some people are worried that AI will take all our jobs. **Although** this fear is understandable, **I still believe** young people can win in this new world, **because** we learn fast and we grew up with technology. The danger is not AI itself — the danger is standing still.

> **To sum up, I've talked about three things:** the new opportunities AI creates, the new skills we need, and the risks we should prepare for. **The main thing to remember is this:** AI will not take your job — but a person who uses AI better than you might.

> **Thank you very much for your attention. If you have any questions, I'd be happy to answer them.**

### 🎭 Câu hỏi Q&A dự kiến (Bài 1)

- **Q:** *Which jobs will AI replace first?*
  **A:** *That's a good question, but I'm not sure about the answer. I think* simple and repeated jobs, like _data entry_, are in danger first. Jobs that need feelings — like nurses or teachers — are much safer.
- **Q:** *Do you use AI for your studies?*
  **A:** *Yes, I do. But what I mean is* — we should use it to learn, not to copy. *In my own experience*, I use AI to check my grammar, and my English is getting better.

# ✍️ BÀI 2 — *Adventure Activities: Advantages and Disadvantages* (≈610 từ)

> Hello everyone! My name is ______. It's great to see you all today.

> Let me start with a question: **Have you ever tried** _bungee jumping_, _rock climbing_, or _zip-lining_? Or are you the kind of person who says, "No way, that's crazy!"? Today, **I'd like to talk about** _adventure activities_ — what they are, their advantages, and their disadvantages. **From my point of view**, they can be wonderful for young people, but only if we stay safe.

> **I've divided my talk into three parts. First**, I'll explain what adventure activities are. **Then, I'll move on to** their advantages. **And finally, I'll discuss** their disadvantages and give some advice.

> **Let's begin by looking at** what adventure activities are. They are activities that give us excitement and a little danger — things like _hiking_ in the mountains, _kayaking_ on rivers, _paragliding_ in the sky, and _canyoning_ down waterfalls. **Believe it or not**, these activities are becoming very popular with young Vietnamese people. **Take** Da Lat, **for example** — every year, thousands of young people travel there for _camping_ and _canyoning_. **As you can see**, adventure is not only for professional athletes anymore; it has become a normal weekend hobby.

> **Let's move on to my second point:** the advantages. **First of all**, adventure activities help us _overcome fear_ and build _confidence_. When you stand at the top of a climbing wall, your legs shake — but when you reach the top, you feel like a hero. **In my own experience**, after my first _rock climbing_ trip, I felt I could do anything — even speak English in front of the class! **Another point is that** these activities teach _teamwork_. **Since** we must watch and help each other, we learn to trust our friends. **What's more**, adventure activities are healthy. **Because** they are exciting, we exercise for hours without feeling bored — it's much better than sitting at home with our phones.

> **That brings me to my last point:** the disadvantages. **The biggest problem is that** these activities can be _risky_. Without good _safety equipment_ and a trained _instructor_, people can get seriously _injured_. Sadly, we sometimes read about accidents in the news, and most of them happen when people don't follow the rules. **In addition**, adventure activities are often _expensive_. One _paragliding_ flight can cost as much as a month of lunch money, and good equipment is not cheap. **And one more thing:** some young people take dangerous selfies just for likes on social media — that's not adventure, that's just a bad idea.

> **On the one hand**, adventure sports give us confidence, health, and wonderful memories. **On the other hand**, one careless moment can cause a serious accident. So what should we do? **In my opinion**, the answer is simple: enjoy them, but be smart. Choose a licensed centre, listen to the _instructor_, check the equipment, and never go alone.

> **To sum up, I've talked about** what adventure activities are, their advantages — confidence, teamwork, and health — and their disadvantages — risk and cost. **The main thing to remember is:** be brave, but be safe. The mountain will still be there tomorrow, so there is no reason to take a stupid risk today.

> **Thank you for listening! If you have any questions, I'd be happy to answer them.**

# ✍️ BÀI 3 — *How Social Media Has Changed Commerce* (≈630 từ)

> Good afternoon everyone, and thank you for being here today. I'm ______.

> Let me start with a quick question: **Have you ever bought** something during a _livestream_? Or maybe you ordered a bubble tea just because you saw it on TikTok? **That's what I'll talk about today** — how social media has changed _commerce_: the world of buying and selling. **In my opinion**, social media hasn't just changed shopping a little — it has changed everything: how we buy, how we sell, and how we trust.

> **I've divided my talk into three parts. First, I'll talk about** how our shopping habits have changed. **Then, I'll move on to** the big opportunities for small businesses. **And finally, I'll discuss** the problems and dangers.

> **Let's begin by looking at** shopping habits. In the past, our parents went to the market or the shopping mall. Today, the market comes to our phones. **For instance**, on TikTok Shop or Shopee Live, people watch a _livestream_, chat with the seller, and buy a product in one minute — at midnight, in their pyjamas! **According to reports**, millions of Vietnamese people now shop this way every single day. **What's more**, before we buy anything, we check _reviews_ and _compare prices_ on social media. **As a result**, _customers_ today have more information and more power than ever before.

> **Now that we've looked at** the buyers, **let's turn to** the sellers. **The key point here is** that social media gives _small businesses_ a huge chance. In the past, if you wanted to open a shop, you needed a lot of money for rent and _advertising_. **However**, today anyone can start a business with just a phone. **Because** advertising on social media is cheap — or even free — a student can sell handmade jewellery, and a farmer can sell fruit directly to customers in the city. **Let me share a personal story.** My aunt makes cakes at home. She has no shop and no staff, but she posts photos on Facebook, customers order in the comments, and she gets orders every day. Social media is her shop window, her advertisement, and her customer service — all in one app. **In addition**, companies now pay _influencers_ to introduce products, **because** young people trust people more than advertisements.

> **That brings me to my last point:** the problems. **Of course**, this new world is not perfect. **The biggest problem is** _fake products_. Some sellers show a beautiful dress in the livestream but send something completely different. **Another problem is** fake _reviews_ — some shops pay people to write nice comments. **As a result**, customers sometimes lose money and, worse, they lose trust. **And one more thing:** shopping is now so easy that many young people buy things they don't need. Easy buying can slowly become _shopping addiction_.

> **Although** these problems are real, **I still believe** social media is good for commerce, **because** it gives small sellers a fair chance and gives buyers more choice and better prices. We just need to be smart: buy from shops with good ratings, read real reviews, and think twice before clicking "buy now".

> **To sum up, I've talked about three things:** new shopping habits, new chances for small businesses, and the risks of online selling. **The main thing to remember is this:** today, every phone is a shop — so buy smart, and sell honestly.

> **Thank you very much for your attention. If you have any questions, I'd be happy to answer them.**

### 🎭 Câu hỏi Q&A dự kiến (Bài 3)

- **Q:** *How can we avoid fake products?*
  **A:** *That's a very interesting question.* *I think* we should buy from shops with many good ratings, read the reviews carefully, and — *the most important thing* — if the price looks too good to be true, it's probably fake!
- **Q:** *Is livestream selling a real job?*
  **A:** *Yes, I believe so.* Some sellers earn more than office workers. *However*, it's not easy — they talk for hours every night, and they need to understand customers very well.

## 🏋️ Cách luyện 1 bài trong 4 ngày

| Ngày | Việc cần làm |
|------|--------------|
| 1 | Đọc to toàn bài 3 lần. Tra & học bảng từ vựng. Gạch chân cụm khung in đậm. |
| 2 | **Thay ví dụ** bằng chuyện thật của bạn (anh họ → bạn của bạn, cô của tôi → người bạn biết). Đọc to bản đã sửa. |
| 3 | Thu âm cả bài, bấm giờ (mục tiêu 4:30–5:30). Nghe lại, sửa các cụm đọc sai (xem File 4, mục 6). |

> 💡 **Mẹo cho bài 5 phút:** đừng cố nhớ từng câu — hãy nhớ **bản đồ 6 dòng**. Cụm từ khung (in đậm) chính là "đường ray"
