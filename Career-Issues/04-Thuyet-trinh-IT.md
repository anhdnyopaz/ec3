# 💻 Topic – Career Issues | File 4: THUYẾT TRÌNH NGÀNH IT (Vấn đề trong nghề IT & cách đối diện)

> **Mục đích:** mở rộng chủ điểm *Career Issues* sang **các vấn đề CỤ THỂ của ngành IT (lập trình / phần mềm)** — biết **nêu vấn đề kỹ thuật**, **xin/cho lời khuyên** và **thuyết trình** về cách xử lý.
> Vẫn dùng đúng 2 vũ khí ngữ pháp của chủ điểm: **Lời khuyên** (should / ought to / had better / why don't you / how about) + **Câu điều kiện loại 1** (If + hiện tại đơn, … will/can/should).
> **Quy ước in chữ:** **cấu trúc lời khuyên** in **đậm** · ***câu điều kiện If*** in đậm-nghiêng · _từ vựng chủ đề IT_ in _nghiêng_.

Trình độ: **A2–B1** (A2 ≈ 75%, B1 ≈ 25%). Mỗi bài thuyết trình dài ~2 phút (≈ 240–270 từ).

# 🗣️ PHẦN A – HỘI THOẠI MẪU (Asking for advice in IT)

## 💬 Hội thoại 1 — *"I can't fix this bug!"* (Xin lời khuyên khi gặp bug khó)

> **An:** Hi Long, you look tired. What's wrong?
> **Long:** I've been fixing _a bug_ for three hours, but the app still _crashes_. I _feel stressed_.
> **An:** I understand. Can I give you some advice? First, you **should** _check the logs_ carefully.
> **Long:** I did, but I don't see the problem.
> **An:** **Why don't you** _debug_ step by step? **How about** _writing a small test_ to find where it breaks?
> **Long:** Good idea. I always try to read all the code at once.
> **An:** That's the problem. You**'d better not** change too many things together, or you will make more _bugs_. ***If*** you _debug_ one part at a time, you **will** find it faster.
> **Long:** Thank you! I **should** also _ask a senior_ when I feel stuck.

> **🔑 Lời khuyên:** should, why don't you, how about, had better not. **Điều kiện:** If you debug one part at a time… **Từ vựng:** bug, crash, check the logs, debug, write a test, ask a senior.

---

## 💬 Hội thoại 2 — *"The deadline is too tight"* (Xin lời khuyên khi deadline gấp)

> **Mai:** Can I ask you for some advice?
> **Lead:** Of course. What's the matter?
> **Mai:** We have _a tight deadline_ this Friday, but the _legacy code_ is very hard to change.
> **Lead:** I see. **It's a good idea to** _break the tasks down_ into small parts. ***If*** you finish the important part first, you **will** feel less _pressure_.
> **Mai:** But I'm afraid we cannot finish everything.
> **Lead:** Then you **ought to** tell me early. ***If*** you tell your manager early, we **can** move the _deadline_ or add help.
> **Mai:** Okay. I don't want to _work overtime_ every night.
> **Lead:** Right. You **should** also _write tests_, **because** ***if*** we skip tests, we **will** get more _bugs_ later.

> **🔑 Lời khuyên:** it's a good idea to, ought to, should. **Điều kiện:** If you finish the important part first…, If you tell your manager early…, If we skip tests… **Từ vựng:** tight deadline, legacy code, break tasks down, write tests, work overtime, bug.

# ✍️ PHẦN B – ĐOẠN VĂN MẪU (Model paragraph, ≈135 từ, A2–B1)

## *A common problem for IT workers and how to solve it*

Many _programmers_ face a common problem: they meet too many _bugs_ and have _a tight deadline_. As a result, they _work overtime_, _feel stressed_, and sometimes get _burnout_.

In my opinion, there are simple ways to deal with this. First, you **should** _break your tasks down_ and _manage your time_ well. ***If*** you do the most important part first, you **will** feel calmer. Second, you **ought to** _write tests_ and _debug_ step by step, **because** ***if*** you check your code early, you **will** _fix bugs_ faster.

Moreover, don't be afraid to _ask a senior_. ***If*** you ask for help, you **can** learn and grow.

In conclusion, ***if*** we _manage our time_, _test our code_, and _keep learning_, we **will** _improve our performance_ and enjoy our work in IT.

> **🔑 Lời khuyên:** should, ought to. **Điều kiện:** If you do the most important part first…, If you check your code early…, If you ask for help…, If we manage our time… **Từ vựng:** bugs, tight deadline, work overtime, burnout, break tasks down, write tests, debug, ask a senior, keep learning, improve performance.

## 🎤 Bài 1 — *How to deal with bugs and stress in IT* (≈255 từ)

> Hello everyone! My name is ______. Today, I'd like to talk about a common problem for people who work in IT: _bugs_ and _stress_ — and how we can deal with them.

> In our job, we write code every day, so we often meet _bugs_ and _errors_. Sometimes the app _crashes_ before _a tight deadline_. Because of this, many _programmers_ _work overtime_, _feel stressed_, and slowly get _burnout_. So, what **should** we do?

> First, we **should** _debug_ step by step. ***If*** you _check the logs_ and test one small part first, you **will** find the problem faster. **Why don't you** _write a small test_ before you fix the code?

> Second, remember to _manage your time_. **It's a good idea to** _break your tasks down_ into small parts. ***If*** you finish the most important part first, you **can** stay calm even with _a tight deadline_.

> Third, don't be afraid to _ask a senior_. ***If*** there is _poor communication_ in the team, you **ought to** ask questions and speak clearly. Nobody knows everything in IT.

> ***Of course***, we cannot remove all _bugs_. ***However***, ***if*** we _keep learning_ and know our _strengths and weaknesses_, we **can** keep improving.

> ***In conclusion***, ***if*** we _debug_ carefully, _manage our time_, and _ask for help_, we **will** _fix bugs_ faster and _improve our performance_. Thank you for listening!

> **🔑 Lời khuyên:** should (×2), why don't you, it's a good idea to, ought to. **Điều kiện:** If you check the logs…, If you finish the most important part…, If there is poor communication…, If we keep learning…, If we debug carefully… **Tương phản:** Of course, However.

## 🎤 Bài 2 — *How to keep learning new technology* (≈250 từ)

> Hello everyone! Today I'd like to talk about a big challenge in IT: _technology_ changes very fast — and how we can keep up with it.

> Every year, there are new tools, new languages, and new frameworks. Because of this, many _developers_ _feel stressed_ and think, "I can never learn everything." Some people even get _imposter syndrome_ and feel they are not good enough. So, what **should** we do?

> First, you **should** _keep learning_ a little every day. ***If*** you study just 30 minutes a day, you **will** improve a lot after one year. **How about** _taking_ a short online course?

> Second, **it's a good idea to** _practice_ with small projects. ***If*** you build something real, you **will** remember the knowledge much better than only reading.

> Third, **why don't you** learn from your team? ***If*** you _ask a senior_ or join a _code review_, you **can** learn from other people's experience.

> ***Of course***, we cannot learn every new tool. ***However***, ***if*** we focus on the basics and know our _strengths and weaknesses_, we **will** feel more confident.

> ***In conclusion***, ***if*** we _keep learning_, _practice_ every day, and learn from our team, we **will** grow in our career and enjoy working in IT. Thank you for listening!

> **🔑 Lời khuyên:** should, how about, it's a good idea to, why don't you. **Điều kiện:** If you study 30 minutes a day…, If you build something real…, If you ask a senior…, If we focus on the basics… **Từ vựng:** technology, imposter syndrome, keep learning, take a course, practice, code review, ask a senior, strengths and weaknesses.

## 🎤 Bài 3 — *What to do when the system crashes* (≈245 từ)

> Hello everyone! Today I'd like to talk about a stressful moment in IT: a _system crash_ — when the app or the server suddenly stops working — and how we should deal with it.

> Imagine it is Friday evening, and the website is _down_. Users cannot log in, and everyone is worried. In this _production incident_, many people _feel stressed_ and _make mistakes_. So, what **should** we do?

> First, you **should** _stay calm_. ***If*** you panic, you **will** _make more mistakes_. Take a deep breath before you touch anything.

> Second, **it's a good idea to** _check the logs_ first. ***If*** you read the error messages, you **can** find the cause faster. **Why don't you** ask the team, "What changed today?"

> Third, remember to _back up the data_. You**'d better not** delete anything quickly, or you will lose important information. ***If*** you have a _backup_, you **can** fix the problem safely.

> Fourth, **you ought to** follow the _incident plan_ and tell your manager. Good _communication_ helps everyone stay calm.

> ***In conclusion***, ***if*** we _stay calm_, _check the logs_, and work as a team, we **will** solve the _incident_ faster and learn from it. After that, we **should** write notes so the same problem **won't** happen again. Thank you for listening!

> **🔑 Lời khuyên:** should (×2), it's a good idea to, why don't you, had better not, ought to. **Điều kiện:** If you panic…, If you read the error messages…, If you have a backup…, If we stay calm… **Từ vựng:** system crash, downtime, production incident, stay calm, check the logs, back up data, incident plan, communication.

## 🧱 KHUNG ĐIỀN của riêng bạn (IT presentation template)

```
[Mở đầu]   Hello everyone! Today I'd like to talk about a problem in IT: ________ .
[Vấn đề]   In our job, we often ________ (meet bugs / have tight deadlines…),
           so many programmers ________ (feel stressed / work overtime / get burnout).
[Giải pháp 1] First, you should ________ . If you ________ , you will ________ .
[Giải pháp 2] Second, it's a good idea to ________ . How about ________ (V-ing)?
[Giải pháp 3] Third, why don't you ________ ? / you ought to ________ .
[Nhượng bộ]  Of course, we cannot ________ . However, if we ________ , we can ________ .
[Kết]       In conclusion, if we ________ , we will improve our performance.
           Thank you for listening!
```

---

## 💡 Mẹo đạt B1 (cho bài IT)

1. Mỗi giải pháp = **1 lời khuyên** (should/why don't you…) + **1 câu điều kiện** (If…, … will/should…).
2. Dùng **ít nhất 2–3 cách khuyên khác nhau** — đừng lặp lại *should* cả bài.
3. Giải thích **thuật ngữ IT** đơn giản cho người nghe (*a bug = a mistake in the code*).
4. Luôn có mạch: **vấn đề kỹ thuật → điều kiện → giải pháp → kết quả tốt** (*fix bugs faster / improve performance*).
5. Chèn 1–2 câu **tương phản** (*Of course… However…*) để nghe "chín" hơn.
6. Nói xong, **đọc to** và bấm giờ ~2 phút.

## ✅ Checklist trước khi thuyết trình (IT)

- [ ] Nêu rõ **1 vấn đề IT** ở đầu (bugs / tight deadline / system crash / new technology…).
- [ ] Giải thích ngắn gọn thuật ngữ cho người nghe không chuyên.
- [ ] Có **≥ 2 cách đưa lời khuyên** khác nhau (should / why don't you / had better / how about / ought to).
- [ ] Có **≥ 2 câu điều kiện loại 1** (If + hiện tại đơn, … will/should…).
- [ ] Dùng **≥ 5 từ vựng IT** (debug, bug, deadline, refactor, logs, backup…).
- [ ] Có **1 câu tương phản** (Of course… However…).
- [ ] Kết bằng **kết quả tích cực** (*fix bugs faster / improve performance / grow in your career*).
- [ ] Nói được **≈ 2 phút** không nhìn giấy.
